# Cross-Layer Research Insights Summary

**Research Synthesis Date:** December 26, 2025
**Framework:** Teresa Torres 6-Layer Problem Definition Framework (Layers 1-3)
**Total Sources Analyzed:** 150+ across all research documents

---

## Executive Summary

This document synthesizes findings across all three research layers to identify convergent insights and strategic implications for the Claude Discovery Hub.

### The Core Discovery

**The Claude Skill Discovery Problem is fundamentally a behavioral problem, not a technical or awareness problem.**

Users know they could do more with Claude. Authors have created thousands of skills. The ecosystem infrastructure exists. Yet discovery fails because:

1. **Layer 1 (Mental Models):** Users frame themselves as "not knowing enough" rather than "not being shown"
2. **Layer 2 (Ecosystem):** The supply side is fragmented with no unified discovery mechanism
3. **Layer 3 (Behavioral):** Efficient workflows actively discourage exploration

---

## Cross-Layer Convergence Points

### Convergence 1: The Invisibility Problem

**Layer 1 Evidence:**
- "99% of Claude 4 Users Don't Know This Feature Exists"
- "Due to Claude Code's CLI-based nature, a lot of stuff is hidden"

**Layer 2 Evidence:**
- Skills are auto-invoked; users never see them working
- No centralized marketplace; skills scattered across GitHub

**Layer 3 Evidence:**
- Discovery requires proactive action
- Current workflows have zero discovery moments

**Unified Insight:** The product architecture makes capabilities invisible by design. Auto-invocation (a feature) creates invisibility (a bug for discovery).

**Strategic Implication:** Discovery Hub must make the invisible visible without disrupting the auto-invocation benefit.

---

### Convergence 2: The "Good Enough" Trap

**Layer 1 Evidence:**
- Users operate at 20% of tool potential and feel satisfied
- The "70% Problem" - reaching 70% quickly, struggling with final 30%

**Layer 2 Evidence:**
- Only 33% of enterprises achieve majority developer adoption
- 76% use AI tools, but only 43% trust them

**Layer 3 Evidence:**
- Status quo bias: 95% never change defaults
- Satisficing: accepting "good enough" over optimal

**Unified Insight:** The combination of partial capability (working at 70%) plus psychological inertia (95% keep defaults) creates a stable equilibrium at sub-optimal usage.

**Strategic Implication:** Discovery Hub must disrupt this equilibrium by making the gap between current and potential painfully visible.

---

### Convergence 3: The Context Paradox

**Layer 1 Evidence:**
- "Every morning, you essentially onboard a new team member from scratch"
- 66,000+ tokens consumed before conversation starts

**Layer 2 Evidence:**
- Token overhead is universal pain point for authors
- Skills compete with each other for context budget

**Layer 3 Evidence:**
- Context preservation is primary workflow optimization
- Users resist anything that "wastes tokens"

**Unified Insight:** Users optimize for context preservation, but that optimization blocks discovery of skills that could reduce context needs.

**Strategic Implication:** Discovery Hub must frame skill discovery as context efficiency improvement, not consumption.

---

### Convergence 4: The Trust Calibration Crisis

**Layer 1 Evidence:**
- Only 43% trust AI accuracy
- Reports of Claude "lying about changes" erode confidence

**Layer 2 Evidence:**
- 62% of AI-generated code is insecure by default
- No verification badges or quality signals

**Layer 3 Evidence:**
- Both overtrust (blind copy-pasting) and undertrust exist
- Trust requires evidence at design, organization, and oversight levels

**Unified Insight:** Trust is miscalibrated in both directions. Users who overtrust create security risks; users who undertrust abandon valuable tools.

**Strategic Implication:** Discovery Hub must provide calibrated trust signals - neither overselling nor underselling skill capabilities.

---

### Convergence 5: The 11-Week Adoption Reality

**Layer 1 Evidence:**
- Breakthrough moment occurs in Week 2-3
- Transformed workflow emerges in Month 1+

**Layer 2 Evidence:**
- Adoption pattern: 4% → 83% → 60% (month 1 → peak → stable)
- Enterprise adoption is "widespread but shallow"

**Layer 3 Evidence:**
- Teams need 11 weeks to fully realize benefits
- Quick wins needed to maintain motivation through learning curve

**Unified Insight:** Skill adoption is not an event but a journey requiring sustained engagement over 2-3 months.

**Strategic Implication:** Discovery Hub must support the full adoption journey, not just initial discovery.

---

## The Unified Problem Statement

Based on cross-layer analysis:

> **Users with efficient Claude Code workflows actively resist discovering skills that could improve those workflows, because the current design makes capabilities invisible, the ecosystem is fragmented, and behavioral economics favor the status quo over exploration.**

### The Three Barriers

| Barrier | Layer | Root Cause |
|---------|-------|------------|
| **Invisibility** | 1 + 2 | Auto-invocation + fragmentation |
| **Inertia** | 1 + 3 | "Good enough" + status quo bias |
| **Distrust** | 2 + 3 | No quality signals + calibration crisis |

---

## Strategic Design Requirements

Based on cross-layer synthesis, Discovery Hub must:

### Requirement 1: Make the Invisible Visible
- Show skills in action ("Using: TDD Skill")
- Surface capabilities at moment of relevance
- Attribution that builds awareness

### Requirement 2: Disrupt the "Good Enough" Equilibrium
- Quantify the gap between current and potential
- Show what peers are achieving
- Make optimization feel low-effort

### Requirement 3: Preserve Context Efficiency
- Skills must be context-efficient, not context-hungry
- Frame discovery as efficiency gain
- One-click adoption without configuration

### Requirement 4: Build Calibrated Trust
- Quality signals that are neither overselling nor underselling
- Verification badges with meaning
- Transparent limitations alongside capabilities

### Requirement 5: Support the 11-Week Journey
- Quick wins in first session
- Progress tracking over weeks
- Social proof and team dynamics

## Contrarian Perspectives to Consider

Research also surfaced important skeptical viewpoints:

**Gary Marcus (Academic Skeptic):**
> "Nobody's going to make much money off it because they're expensive to run, and everybody has the same product."

**CTO Survey Finding:**
> 16 of 18 CTOs reported "production disasters directly caused by AI-generated code"

**METR Study:**
> Developers using AI tools were 19% slower than those without, yet predicted they would be 24% faster.

**Implication:** Discovery Hub must acknowledge real limitations, not oversell capabilities. Calibrated expectations build sustainable trust.

This captures:
- **Layer 1:** Addressing the black box / opacity problem
- **Layer 2:** Consolidating fragmented ecosystem
- **Layer 3:** Making the invisible visible without disruption
