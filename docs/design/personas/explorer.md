# The Explorer

> **Navigation**: [Design Index](../index.md) > [Personas](./index.md) > Explorer

**Archetype:** The Curious Power User

> *"I want to know what's possible, not just what's practical."*

---

## Demographics

- 3-7 years development experience
- Uses Claude Code for 2+ hours daily
- Active on dev Twitter/Mastodon, follows tooling trends
- Early adopter of new technologies

---

## Goals

| Functional | Emotional |
|------------|-----------|
| Discover skills before peers | Feel ahead of the curve |
| Understand ecosystem breadth | Experience exploration joy |
| Find hidden gems | Feel satisfaction from curation |
| Stay updated on new releases | Avoid fear of missing out |

---

## Fears and Anxieties

- Missing something important that everyone else knows
- Wasting time on skills that don't deliver
- The ecosystem moving faster than they can track
- Being seen as behind the times

---

## Current Behavior and Workarounds

- Browses GitHub trending weekly
- Follows 10+ skill authors on social media
- Maintains personal "to try" list
- Shares discoveries with colleagues to gauge reactions

---

## Trust Triggers (What Builds Confidence)

- Seeing download counts and star history
- Author reputation and track record
- Detailed changelogs showing active development
- Community discussions and reviews
- Connections to other skills they already trust

---

## Delight Triggers (What Creates Joy)

- Discovering something before it trends
- Finding a skill that solves a problem they thought unsolvable
- Seeing their installed skills reflected in community patterns
- Unexpected connections between skills

---

## Preferred Entry Points

1. **Web browser** for casual browsing
2. **Terminal** for deep search
3. **Public profiles** to see what respected developers use

---

## Red Flags That Cause Abandonment

- Search that returns only popular/obvious results
- No way to filter by "new this week"
- Recommendations that don't surface anything novel
- System that feels like it's pushing mainstream choices

---

## Design Implications

### For Discovery Features
- Surface "new this week" and "rising" skills prominently
- Show when skills are trending before they become mainstream
- Enable filtering by recency and growth rate

### For Search
- Support advanced filters for author, recency, category
- Show novelty indicators in search results
- Don't just show popular results first

### For Recommendations
- Include at least one "unexpected" recommendation
- Show connections to other skills they use
- Explain what makes each recommendation interesting

### For Social Features
- Enable following authors and stacks
- Show what respected developers are using
- Support sharing discoveries easily
